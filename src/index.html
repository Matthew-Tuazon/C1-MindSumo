Average Biker Distance

-As previously mentioned before, there have been almost over 50000 bike trips where the total distance traveled is almost 350000 miles!
-To be exact, the calculated total distance is about 349188 miles. The calculated number of total trips is 49832 trips.
-Round trips were excluded from this data set due to round trips calculating total distance traveled as 0.
-This leaves us with the average distance a biker has traveled to be a little over 7 miles each!

The total distance traveled is essentially going from New Jersey to Los Angeles 121 Times!

Regular Bikers

The amount of regular bike riders that include bike sharing as a part of the commute is the number of bikers who purchase a pass. This is due to the fact that their ride is probably not a one time occurence. We can conclude that a rider would utilize their pass regularly during daily commutes.

Monthly Pass Holders

Looking at our dataset, we were able to conclude that out of all the trips, 81304 of them held a monthly pass.

Walk-Up Trips

Looking at our dataset, we were able to conclude that out of all the trips, 41224 of them were walk-up trips.

Flex Pass Holders

Looking at our dataset, we were able to conclude that out of all the trips, 9517 of them held a flex pass.

Staff Annual Trips

Looking at our dataset, we were able to conclude that out of all the trips, 382 of them were members of the Staff Annual passes.

Regular Bikers Calculation

To previously explain what I said before, I visualized the calculation for you.
I thought I'd save you a couple of seconds checking if my calculation was actually right.
The way I calculated the number of Regular Biker trips was just by adding the number of Monthly Passes used on trips, the number of Flex Passes used on trips, and the number of Staff Annual trips.
Using this calculation, we can come to the conclusion that there were 91203 trips that included regular bike riders!

Now Here Are Some Much More Interesting Metrics I Found in the Dataset

How does ridership change within the four seasons?

Spring
  • In the Spring time, the most common type of pass used are Monthly passes, amounting to 8451 used.
  • The next most common type of pass used are the Walk-Up passes, where 4432 of trips were Walk-Ups in the Spring!
  • Following Walk-Up passes, Flex passes were used 1023 times out of all the trips in the Spring!
  • Lastly, there were 0 Staff Annual passes that were considered during the Spring time.
  • The average Spring trip duration was around 30 minutes!
Summer
  • In the Summer time, the most common type of pass used are Monthly passes, amounting to 22548 used.
  • The next most common type of pass used are the Walk-Up passes, where 9772 of trips were Walk-Ups in the Summer!
  • Following Walk-Up passes, Flex passes were used 3253 times out of all the trips in the Summer!
  • Lastly, there were 0 Staff Annual passes that were considered during the Summer time.
  • The average Summer trip duration was around 24 minutes!
Fall
  • In the Fall time, the most common type of pass used are Monthly passes, amounting to 30789 used.
  • The next most common type of pass used are the Walk-Up passes, where 18071 of trips were Walk-Ups in the Fall!
  • Following Walk-Up passes, Flex passes were used 3345 times out of all the trips in the Fall!
  • Lastly, there were 250 Staff Annual passes that were considered during the Fall time.
  • The average Fall trip duration was around 25 minutes!
Winter
  • In the Winter time, the most common type of pass used are Monthly passes, amounting to 19516 used.
  • The next most common type of pass used are the Walk-Up passes, where 8949 of trips were Walk-Ups in the Winter!
  • Following Walk-Up passes, Flex passes were used 1896 times out of all the trips in the Winter!
  • Lastly, there were 132 Staff Annual passes that were considered during the Winter time.
  • The average Winter trip duration was around 28 minutes!

Using this information, we can come to the conclusion that some seasons are more popular in some aspects than the others.
For example, in the Fall, Monthly passes are at the highest. Maybe we can try having a promotion during lower monthly pass frequency seasons like Spring.
We also notice that Fall is just a popular season in general, where even Walk-Ups are at it's highest.
Maybe the company can create group promotions to encourage more riders to come out and utilize bike sharing!

Trip Route Category-Passholder Combinations

Depending on the type of trip (One-Way or Round Trip), there are trends that correlate alongside the type of pass used.
From the data, we notice that One-Way trips are much more popular than Round Trips!

Round Trips

For Round Trips the following passes used counters are:

  1. Monthly Passes Count - 3772
  2. Flex Passes Count - 483
  3. Staff Annual Passes Count - 83
  4. Walk-Up Passes Count - 0

One-Way Trips

For One-Way Trips the following passes used counters are:

  1. Monthly Passes Count - 77532
  2. Flex Passes Count - 9034
  3. Staff Annual Passes Count - 299
  4. Walk-Up Passes Count - 0

For Round Trips, we acknowledged that they are less popular than One-Way trips. This might be due to the fact that
there are a ton of regular commuter bikers. This means that those commuter bikers are more likely to
only go One-Way to work and One-Way back instead of going on a Round Trip.
